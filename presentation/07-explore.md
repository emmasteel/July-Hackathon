# Explore — understand before you build

Explore is how we find what's possible, then pick a path worth taking. It's divergent thinking: going wide before going deep. Study what exists, sit with the problem long enough to know *who* you're solving it for.

## In the hack, that's

- Read your **use case** and the **user stories** in your team folder.
- Look at the starting skeleton and the **fixtures** — the sample data you'll build against.
- For accessibility, observe the real government site in a browser to inform your findings (but automate only against fixtures).

<div class="callout">
This phase is done when you can answer: do I know enough to pick a direction?
</div>

<p class="pixel">A good use of Copilot here: "Explain this codebase / these stories to me."</p>
