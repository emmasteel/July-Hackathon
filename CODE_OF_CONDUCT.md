# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We want the GitHub Copilot Hackathon to be a welcoming, respectful, and inclusive
experience for **everyone** — regardless of role, seniority, coding experience, background,
identity, or ability. This is a learning event: mixed skill levels are expected and valued.

## Our standards

Examples of behaviour that helps everyone learn:

- Being patient and kind, especially with people newer to coding or the tooling.
- Assuming good intent and asking clarifying questions.
- Giving credit; sharing what you learned from Copilot with your team.
- Making space for BAs, testers, and security specialists to lead the parts they know best.
- Accepting constructive feedback gracefully.

Examples of unacceptable behaviour:

- Harassment, discrimination, or demeaning comments of any kind.
- Gatekeeping ("real developers would…"), belittling questions, or dismissing non-coders.
- Sharing others' private information without consent.
- Sustained disruption of sessions or deliberate intimidation.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all hackathon spaces — physical rooms, chat channels, the
repository (issues, PRs, discussions), and any related social events.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behaviour, please contact a facilitator during the
event, or reach out privately to:

- **Event contact:** Emma Steel — Solution Engineer, Microsoft (emma.steel@microsoft.com)

All reports will be handled discreetly and taken seriously.

## Enforcement

Facilitators may take any action they deem appropriate, up to and including asking a
participant to leave the event, to keep the space safe and welcoming.
